ABOUT US

Hospitals have been an essential part of human societies for centuries, serving as places where the sick and injured could receive care and treatment. The history of hospitals can be traced back to ancient times, where temples were often used as places for healing. In ancient Greece, the Asclepion temples were dedicated to the god of medicine, and people went there seeking healing. During the Middle Ages, the Christian church played a significant role in providing medical care. Monasteries and convents served as hospitals, and religious orders such as the Knights Hospitaller provided care for pilgrims and travelers. It wasn't until the 17th century that hospitals began to be established as separate institutions, with the first general hospital being founded in Paris in 1656.
